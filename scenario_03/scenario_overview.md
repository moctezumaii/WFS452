# Scenario 03: Black Crappie Size Structure and Harvest Management

## Site Description

- **Water body:** Silver Creek Impoundment
- **State/Region:** Central Plains
- **Surface area:** 420 acres
- **Mean depth:** 9 feet
- **Maximum depth:** 24 feet

## Main Task

Silver Creek Impoundment is a shallow, eutrophic impoundment that supports a popular black crappie fishery. Biologists have noticed that the size structure of the black crappie population appears to have shifted toward smaller individuals over the past several years, raising concerns about overharvest or density-dependent growth limitation. Your team will analyze length-frequency data, size structure indices, and available harvest information to diagnose the problem and develop a management plan that addresses crappie size structure and angler satisfaction.

## Biological Background

Black crappie are the primary sport fish in Silver Creek Impoundment. The fish community also includes channel catfish (stocked), bluegill, green sunfish, common carp, gizzard shad, and freshwater drum. Aquatic vegetation is sparse due to high turbidity from wind-driven sediment resuspension and nutrient loading from agricultural runoff. Crappie recruitment appears to be variable, with occasional strong year classes followed by several weak ones.

## Management Context

- Crappie fishing is the main recreational use of the impoundment; anglers travel from surrounding counties.
- Current regulations allow a 15-fish daily bag limit with no minimum length limit.
- Anglers have expressed dissatisfaction with the proportion of harvestable-size fish.
- The watershed is predominantly agricultural, contributing nutrient and sediment loads.
- Water level management is partially controlled by an outlet structure.
- A local conservation district has expressed interest in partnering on watershed improvements.

## Management Goal Prompt

Your team must propose a long-range management goal that addresses black crappie size structure, harvest sustainability, and the quality of the angling experience at Silver Creek Impoundment.

## Datasets Included

- **crappie_length_frequency.csv**: Length-frequency data for black crappie collected during fall trap net surveys from 2012 to 2025.
- **crappie_harvest_survey.csv**: Voluntary creel/harvest survey data from 2015 to 2025, including angler effort, catch, harvest, and satisfaction ratings.

## Minimum Analyses to Consider

- Construct length-frequency histograms by year or year groups.
- Calculate proportional size distribution (PSD) and PSD of preferred and memorable size fish.
- Examine trends in PSD and mean length over the monitoring period.
- Summarize harvest rates, angler effort, and catch rates from the creel data.
- Evaluate whether harvest pressure or density-dependent growth is a more likely explanation for size structure changes.
- Explore relationships between CPUE and mean length or PSD.
- Compare crappie size structure benchmarks from published literature with your observed values.
