# Scenario 01: Largemouth Bass Population Assessment

## Site Description

- **Water body:** Pine Bluff Reservoir
- **State/Region:** Southeastern United States
- **Surface area:** 1,250 acres
- **Mean depth:** 12 feet
- **Maximum depth:** 38 feet

## Main Task

Pine Bluff Reservoir has historically supported a popular largemouth bass fishery, but recent angler complaints suggest that the average size of bass has declined over the past decade. Agency biologists have conducted annual electrofishing surveys since 2014 and periodic creel surveys. Your team will analyze these data to assess the current status of the largemouth bass population and develop a management plan that addresses the concerns of anglers and the long-term health of the fishery.

## Biological Background

Largemouth bass are the primary predator in Pine Bluff Reservoir. The forage base includes bluegill, threadfin shad, and gizzard shad. Aquatic vegetation is present but limited to shallow coves. The reservoir was impounded in 1985 and experienced strong initial bass production during the first decade. Water levels fluctuate moderately due to downstream water supply releases.

## Management Context

- Local bass clubs and recreational anglers are the primary user groups.
- Current regulations include a 14-inch minimum length limit and a 5-fish daily bag limit for largemouth bass.
- Anglers have reported catching more small bass and fewer quality-size fish in the past 3 to 4 years.
- There is no evidence of invasive species at this time.
- Shoreline development has increased gradually, reducing some natural riparian habitat.

## Management Goal Prompt

Your team must propose a long-range management goal that addresses the perceived decline in largemouth bass size structure and the expectations of recreational anglers at Pine Bluff Reservoir.

## Datasets Included

- **bass_electrofishing.csv**: Annual fall electrofishing survey data for largemouth bass from 2014 to 2025, including individual fish total length (mm) and weight (g), plus catch per unit effort by year.
- **bass_age_growth.csv**: Age and growth data from otolith samples collected in selected years, including age at capture, total length, and back-calculated lengths at age.

## Minimum Analyses to Consider

- Summarize CPUE trends over time.
- Construct length-frequency histograms by year or grouped years.
- Calculate proportional size distribution (PSD) and related indices across years.
- Examine weight-length relationships and relative weight (Wr).
- Estimate growth using von Bertalanffy or other growth models if age data allow.
- Compare observed size structure to management benchmarks for largemouth bass.
- Identify potential causes of changes in size structure using the data and published literature.
