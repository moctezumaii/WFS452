# Scenario 06: Invasive Species and Water Quality in a Small Lake

## Site Description

- **Water body:** Mirror Lake
- **State/Region:** Mid-Atlantic
- **Surface area:** 185 acres
- **Mean depth:** 10 feet
- **Maximum depth:** 28 feet

## Main Task

Mirror Lake is a small, moderately productive natural lake that has experienced two major stressors in recent years: the establishment of an invasive common carp population and a trend of declining water quality linked to nutrient loading from surrounding residential and agricultural land use. Agency biologists have been monitoring the fish community, water quality, and aquatic vegetation since 2010. Your team will analyze these datasets to assess how invasive carp and nutrient enrichment are affecting the fishery and develop a management plan that addresses both stressors while maintaining recreational fishing opportunities.

## Biological Background

The native fish community includes bluegill, largemouth bass, channel catfish, and yellow bullhead. Common carp were first detected in 2013 and have become increasingly abundant. Carp are believed to be degrading water quality through bioturbation (stirring up bottom sediments) and uprooting submerged aquatic vegetation. Aquatic vegetation coverage has declined from approximately 40% of the littoral zone in 2010 to less than 15% in recent surveys. Algal blooms have become more frequent.

## Management Context

- Mirror Lake is a popular fishing destination for families and beginning anglers.
- A lakeside community association of approximately 120 homeowners is concerned about water quality, property values, and the aesthetic condition of the lake.
- Current fishing regulations are standard statewide limits.
- The county soil and water conservation district has offered technical assistance for nutrient management plans on adjacent farmland.
- A commercial carp removal operator has been consulted but costs are substantial.
- State invasive species funding may be available through a competitive grant program.

## Management Goal Prompt

Your team must propose a long-range management goal that addresses the impacts of invasive common carp, nutrient enrichment, and their combined effects on the fish community and recreational value of Mirror Lake.

## Datasets Included

- **fish_community.csv**: Annual fyke net survey data for all species from 2010 to 2025, including species, count, and individual lengths where available.
- **water_quality.csv**: Monthly water quality measurements from May through October (2010 to 2025), including Secchi depth, total phosphorus, total nitrogen, dissolved oxygen profiles, and chlorophyll-a.
- **vegetation_survey.csv**: Aquatic vegetation coverage surveys conducted every two years from 2010 to 2024, with transect-level percent cover by species group.

## Minimum Analyses to Consider

- Summarize trends in common carp CPUE and biomass over time.
- Examine changes in native fish species CPUE before and after carp establishment.
- Summarize trends in water quality parameters (Secchi, TP, TN, chlorophyll-a, DO).
- Examine relationships between carp abundance and water quality.
- Summarize aquatic vegetation coverage trends and relate them to carp abundance and water quality.
- Calculate community composition metrics before and after carp invasion.
- Identify potential management actions for carp control from published literature.
- Assess how water quality improvements might benefit native fish populations.
