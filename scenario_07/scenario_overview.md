# Scenario 07: Urban Reservoir Mixed Warmwater Fishery

## Site Description

- **Water body:** Millstone Reservoir
- **State/Region:** South-central United States
- **Surface area:** 950 acres
- **Mean depth:** 11 feet
- **Maximum depth:** 32 feet

## Main Task

Millstone Reservoir is a flood-control impoundment located on the edge of a growing metropolitan area. The reservoir supports a mixed warmwater fishery with largemouth bass, channel catfish, white crappie, and hybrid striped bass (stocked). As the surrounding urban area expands, the reservoir faces increasing pressure from both anglers and non-angling users (kayakers, swimmers, shoreline picnickers). Stormwater runoff has increased nutrient and sediment inputs, and periodic fish kills have been reported during late summer. Your team will analyze fish population, environmental, and user survey data to develop a management plan that sustains the fishery while addressing urban growth pressures.

## Biological Background

Largemouth bass and channel catfish are naturally reproducing. White crappie recruitment is highly variable. Hybrid striped bass are stocked every other year to diversify angling opportunities but do not reproduce. Gizzard shad are the dominant forage species and periodically experience large die-offs during cold winters. Blue-green algae blooms occur during July and August in most years, occasionally prompting swimming advisories. Threadfin shad were stocked in the early 2000s but have not persisted after severe winters.

## Management Context

- The reservoir is managed jointly by the city parks department and the state wildlife agency.
- Angling is the second most popular recreational activity after trail use.
- A local bass club, a catfish anglers association, and a kayak club have all expressed opinions about management priorities.
- Urban stormwater management is handled by the city, and coordination has been limited.
- A fish consumption advisory for mercury is in effect for largemouth bass longer than 15 inches.
- Bank fishing access is limited; two boat ramps are available but crowded on weekends.
- The city has allocated a modest annual budget for fisheries enhancement.

## Management Goal Prompt

Your team must propose a long-range management goal that balances fishery quality, urban development pressures, multiple user groups, and environmental constraints at Millstone Reservoir.

## Datasets Included

- **reservoir_fish_survey.csv**: Annual fall electrofishing and gill net survey data from 2012 to 2025 for all major species, including counts, lengths, and CPUE by gear type.
- **reservoir_environment.csv**: Monthly environmental monitoring data (May through October, 2012 to 2025) including dissolved oxygen profiles, Secchi depth, surface temperature, and algal bloom indicators.
- **user_survey.csv**: Biennial user intercept survey data (2014, 2016, 2018, 2020, 2022, 2024) covering user type, activity, satisfaction, and management priorities.
- **hybrid_striped_bass_stocking.csv**: Stocking records for hybrid striped bass from 2010 to 2025.

## Minimum Analyses to Consider

- Summarize CPUE trends for each major species by year and gear type.
- Construct length-frequency distributions for bass, catfish, and crappie.
- Calculate PSD for species where size structure data are adequate.
- Summarize dissolved oxygen conditions, especially late-summer hypoxia at depth.
- Evaluate the frequency and timing of algal blooms using chlorophyll-a and Secchi data.
- Summarize user demographics, satisfaction trends, and stated management priorities.
- Assess whether hybrid striped bass stocking is contributing to angler catch and satisfaction.
- Identify conflicts between user groups and potential management solutions.
- Review published literature on urban reservoir fisheries management.
