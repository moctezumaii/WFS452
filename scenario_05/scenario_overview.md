# Scenario 05: Walleye Harvest and Creel Survey Management

## Site Description

- **Water body:** Lake Sandhill
- **State/Region:** Northern Great Plains
- **Surface area:** 3,800 acres
- **Mean depth:** 14 feet
- **Maximum depth:** 42 feet

## Main Task

Lake Sandhill is a large, wind-swept natural lake with a historically productive walleye fishery. Walleye are maintained through a combination of natural reproduction and periodic supplemental stocking. A comprehensive creel survey program has been in place since 2012, providing detailed information on angling effort, catch, harvest, and angler demographics. Recent creel data suggest that total walleye harvest has increased while catch rates of larger fish have declined. Your team will analyze the creel survey data alongside standardized gill net survey results to develop a management plan that balances harvest opportunity with population sustainability.

## Biological Background

Walleye are the most sought-after species at Lake Sandhill. Yellow perch, white bass, and freshwater drum are also present and contribute to angler catch. Walleye natural reproduction occurs in rocky shoreline areas but is variable from year to year. Fingerling walleye have been stocked at irregular intervals (2014, 2016, 2019, 2022). Forage includes young-of-year yellow perch, shiners, and fathead minnows.

## Management Context

- Walleye fishing generates significant economic activity for the surrounding rural community.
- Current regulations include a 15-inch minimum length limit and a 4-fish daily bag limit for walleye.
- A special regulation (protected slot of 17 to 22 inches) was discussed but not implemented.
- Ice fishing accounts for a substantial portion of annual walleye harvest.
- Lakeshore cabin owners and a resort association are vocal stakeholders.
- Water levels have fluctuated due to drought and wet cycles, affecting spawning habitat availability.

## Management Goal Prompt

Your team must propose a long-range management goal that addresses the sustainability of walleye harvest, angler satisfaction, and the economic importance of the fishery at Lake Sandhill.

## Datasets Included

- **walleye_creel.csv**: Creel survey data from 2012 to 2025 including angler effort, catch, harvest, and satisfaction ratings across open-water and ice-fishing seasons.
- **walleye_gillnet.csv**: Standardized fall gill net survey data for walleye from 2012 to 2025, including individual fish lengths and CPUE.
- **walleye_stocking.csv**: Walleye stocking records from 2010 to 2025, including year, number stocked, mean size, and strain.

## Minimum Analyses to Consider

- Summarize annual angling effort (angler-hours) and total harvest from creel data.
- Calculate catch per unit effort (CPUE) for walleye from creel data and from gill net data.
- Compare open-water versus ice-fishing harvest contributions.
- Examine trends in size of walleye in the harvest and in gill net samples.
- Assess whether stocking events correlate with subsequent year-class strength.
- Summarize angler satisfaction trends over time.
- Calculate harvest rate relative to estimated population size (if population estimates can be derived).
- Compare observed harvest metrics to published guidelines for sustainable walleye harvest.
